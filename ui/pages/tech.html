System Architecture
01

Capacitive Discharge Stage

A high-voltage capacitor accumulates a precisely metered energy charge before each ignition event. Unlike inductive systems, discharge energy is not limited by coil saturation — it is set digitally by the controller for every cycle.

02

Resonant Energy Injection

A high-voltage half-bridge converter actively pumps additional energy into the primary winding during the spark discharge. This sustains and extends the plasma channel beyond what a passive CDI can achieve, improving combustion stability under lean or high-pressure conditions.

03

Software Spark Control

The 32-bit controller sets four independent parameters per ignition event: spark energy, discharge duration, primary winding energy and plasma channel shape. Parameters update in real time based on engine feedback without any hardware change.

04

Multi-Channel Output

Four fully independent ignition channels, each with its own profile. Supports sequential, wasted-spark and direct-fire configurations. Channel-to-channel timing resolution is below 1 µs.
